From the microscopic point of view of the nanovirus, Portia and every other affected species on the planet are merely vectors for the onward transmission of the virus’s own evolving genes.

I think this is a core thesis of Richard Dawkins' gene-centered view of evolution. The genes are not selfish per-say; rather, the genes that are passed on are the ones whose evolutionary consequences serve their own implicit interest in being replicated, not necessarily those of the organism.
