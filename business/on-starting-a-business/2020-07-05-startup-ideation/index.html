Getting Startup Ideas

Dated Jul 5, 2020; last modified on Sun, 05 Jul 2020

  • How to Get Startup Ideas []
  • References

How to Get Startup Ideas []

Err on the side of having competitors, but make sure that you have a thesis that everyone else is overlooking, e.g. Google wasn’t averse to users leaving google.com ASAP.

The Startup Idea Matrix, ( B2B Edition , B2C Edition ) is a good start for knowing who to google.

Big companies tend to be slow at changing their approach. You’d think that Yahoo! would clean up their landing page the moment they realized Google was becoming a dominant search engine.

Take classes in different areas in lieu of taking entrepreneurship classes. Chances are that there are problems ripe for a software approach.

Sure, ideas are a dime a dozen, but I don’t think well-researched and thought-out ideas are. Knowing all the CS in the world doesn’t help if you don’t have a good problem statement to apply it to.

Don’t try to think of startup ideas. Instead, notice problems that you’re facing yourself. Be an expert in that market.

Live in the future. Strive to notice what is currently missing, e.g. PG in the 90s noticed that hand-built online stores of custom HTML pages would have to be generated by software soon.

Living in Seattle sort of helps me guess what Kenya’s tech-laden future may hold. For instance, retailers like CVS have lots of loyalty programs. At some point, won’t Kenya’s retailers need those? After all, those retail programs aim to increase customer loyalty and drive sales - and Kenyan retailers should care about that too.

Work on projects that seem like they’d be cool. Things that already exist won’t seem as interesting to build.

Between something that has a large # of people want a small amount and something that a small # of people want a large amount, go for the second.
