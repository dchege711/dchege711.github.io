In 2018, $273b was spent on digital ads. But are ad platforms any good at manipulating us?

Tadelis, Econ Prof at UCB stint @ eBay:

Consultants: paying for sponsored links for people that searched for ‘eBay’ was a $245.6m return, not a $20m expense.

Selection Effect: What if the ad clickers were going to use eBay anyway? Consultants: We use Lagrange multipliers to weed out that effect.

Tadelis makes eBay stop the ‘eBay’ ad at MSN. People who used the paid links were now using the ordinary links. Tadelis halted Google ads on ‘eBay’, ‘shoes’, ‘shirts’ and ‘glassware’. The effect of pulling the ads was insignificant.

Conclusion

The number of clicks, sales and downloads that occur after an ad is viewed don’t distinguish between the selection effect and the advertising effect.

It is in the best interest of a firm like eBay to know whether its campaigns are profitable, but not so for eBay’s marketing department. Marketers are often most successful at marketing their own marketing.
