04. Proper Habits and Methods of Work

Dated Jan 1, 1917; last modified on Sat, 25 Apr 2020

Pick the best book. New to a subject? Start with the elementary one.

Study a few subjects at once, but not too many. The mind needs change of occupation, not idleness.

Weigh carefully before taking up or dropping a study. Read much but not many books.

Fix your mind intensely on one thing for a time long enough to make progress.

Apply what you're studying. Book knowledge is of little value in itself.

Review the main points and summaries frequently.

Set times for recreative study, e.g. cards, chess, billiards.

Exercise. A healthy body is necessary for top mental performance.
