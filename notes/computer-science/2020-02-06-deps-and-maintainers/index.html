Sample Crises in OSS

left-pad: trivial library that was removed from npm and was unknowingly present in thousands of dependency graphs.

event-stream: maintainer transferred ownership to an unknown individual who added crypto mining

heartbleed, in which a bug in a critical security library caused mass upgrades and panic

DeVault's Take

Asking how to prioritize attention, donations & patches for thousands of OSS library dependencies shouldn't be a problem. Why have thousands of dependencies in the first place?

For most of his projects, DeVault can list the entire dependency graph. He knows their maintainers and has personal relations with them - and therefore knows of typical OSS problems in advance. He patches in and refers them to companies and sustainable grant sources.

Y'all should see some of my package.json files. They're nuts! Sometimes, the dependency is an entire project by itself, so why should I do it?
