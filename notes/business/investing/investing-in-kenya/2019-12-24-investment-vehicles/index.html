Investment Vehicles in Kenya

Last modified: Wed, 25 Dec 2019 11:33:48 PST

Investing through Groups (Chamas)

www.kaig.org has developed a Chama Handbook to boost accountability and profitability.

But chamas have a higher chance of being guided by friendships than profitability. For instance, how do I go about picking a chama? It's most likely a referral from a friend who's already in a chama.

Kenya USA Diaspora Sacco

  • Mavuno Term Deposit pays 8% interest for >1m KES and <2m KES deposited for a year.
    • They do treasury bills & bonds, money market funds and deposit accounts.
  • Advises members on which real estate properties to buy
    • Value Prop: clean titles, legitimate owners, fairly priced and projected to appreciate, ensure amenities are in place and negotiated financing plans.

      I'm not bullish on real estate in Kenya. Seems like a bad lemons market. There's the prevailing belief that land will always appreciate. Maybe they're right, maybe it's 2008 all over again.

  • The rest of their investment options are in real estate.

Cytonn Investments

  • Offers high yield solutions (CHYS, LLP) for diaspora
    • 18% over the last 1 year (1m KES min), in an income sharing agreement.
      • CHYS gets a guaranteed return. Their investment arm gets the rest.

So what's the effective rate for me? Also, I asked a friend about Cytonn and she wasn't optimistic about them. Apparently, the real estate branch is slowing down due to capped interest rates for bank loans, hence the drive to get their revenues elsewhere.
