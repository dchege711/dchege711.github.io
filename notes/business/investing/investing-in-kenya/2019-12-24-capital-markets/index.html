Investing in Capital Markets

Last modified: Wed, 25 Dec 2019 11:33:09 PST

The Stock Market

To trade in the NSE, open a CDS account with a CDA (some commercial banks, stockbrokers, investment banks).

In 2006, an Automatic Trading System was introduced so that orders are matched automatically and executed by stockbrokers on a first-come first-served basis. Govt bonds have also been added to the ATS. Securities lending, short-selling and same day turnaround trades are not yet permitted. (kestrelcapital.com)

I guess that's why quant funds are not a thing in Kenya.

As of Dec 25th, 2019, the YTD return of the NSE 20 was -7.82% (bloomberg.com). The 5 year picture is bleaker - the NSE lost 48% while the S&P 500 gained 55%.

Consequently, why would a rational investor put money into the NSE 20 instead of the S&P 500? I'm biased to look for gems because I'm Kenyan. This performance pokes holes in the ‘Kenya's economy is rapidly growing’ narrative. Is it the place to be as an investor?

The Bonds Market

To invest in bonds, open a CDS account with the Central Bank of Kenya.

  • Treasury Bills: < 1 year, min 100k KES
  • Treasury Bonds: 1 - 30 years, min 50k KES

It seems like bills and bonds are more reliable for the Kenyan market. Kenya USA Diaspora Sacco and Cytonn Investments do bills and bonds for diaspora (c13u.com).
