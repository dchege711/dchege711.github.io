07. Dr. J

Dated May 21, 2018; last modified on Tue, 28 Jan 2020

  • Walgreens
  • In Comes Safeway

June 2010 - Jan 2011: FB's private valuation doubles to $50b. “Angry Birds” dramatic App Store sales. Interest rates were ~zero, making hedge funds consider private startups.

Walgreens

Dr. J - Walgreens Innovation. Theranos machines could open up a big new revenue stream. Walgreens agrees to prepurchase $50m of Theranos cartridges and loan Theranos $25m. Gets Walgreens CFO to fast track deal.

Commercially ready lab; devices can do 192 blood tests:

  • Half of those tests couldn't be done with Edison's chemiluminescent immunoassays.
  • Theranos never showed the lab nor sent the results of the vitamin D test!

We'll perform laboratory-developed tests (less regulated):

  • Theranos unwilling to a 50-patient trial with Stanford Hospital.

Theranos's system had been comprehensively validated over the last 7 years by 10/15 largest pharma companies:

  • No one in the pharma companies could verify Theranos’ claims.

Johns Hopkins University's Medical School had reviewed Theranos's tech:

  • JHU's letter had a disclaimer that it doesn't signify an endorsement.

Walgreens: we can't risk a scenario where CVS has a deal with them in 6 months and it ends up being real.

It's interesting that these concerns were raised within Walgreen by junior employees. FOMO is one helluva drug.

In Comes Safeway

Safeway was also in the loop. Burd, CEO, wanted to cut medical costs, while Renda, EVP, could relate since her husband had daily blood draws battling lung cancer.

Safeway agreed to loan Theranos $30m and redesign stores to accomodate Theranos clinics. Safeway would have exclusivity in supermarkets, while Walgreens would have it in drugstores.

And that's how Elizabeth secured $55m for Theranos. Talk about bringing home the bacon without Masayoshi Son's help!
