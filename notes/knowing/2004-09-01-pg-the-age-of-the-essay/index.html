The Age of the Essay [Paul Graham]

Dated Sep 1, 2004; last modified on Sun, 22 Mar 2020

The Age of the Essay. Paul Graham. http://www.paulgraham.com/essay.html. 2004-09-01.

The purpose of an essay is to figure something out. Ergo, you can't begin with a statement.

Expressing ideas helps you form them. Writing something that others might read forces you to think well.

Writing also helps me code better. When I have a nasty bug, it's easy to try out so many ideas such that you lose track of what you've learned. I counter this by recording the ideas that I try out and the result. Sure, it makes me slower, but I end up systematically addressing the bug. And if I'm unable to fix the bug, I can hand off my investigation to someone else.

Think of the essay as an idea graph. At each node, branch out to the most interesting related idea.

To generate surprising insights, write about things that you've thought of a lot, and be more observant.
