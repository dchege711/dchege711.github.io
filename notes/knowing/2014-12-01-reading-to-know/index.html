How to Read and Know [Paul Graham, Robert Heaton]

Dated Dec 1, 2014; last modified on Sun, 22 Mar 2020

How to Read. Robert Heaton. https://robertheaton.com/2018/06/25/how-to-read/. https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=17424652. Jun 25, 2018.
Know. Paul Graham. http://www.paulgraham.com/know.html. Dec 1, 2014.

Choose your content. Some books [and articles] are not worth the effort.

Information overload is one of my major obstacles to reading to know. There are so many pieces out there. I'm biased towards highly upvoted HN submissions. My RSS feeds ran out of control a while back - my queue has +400 pending articles :-/

Reading and experience train your model of the world. Re-reading a book at a different time compiles differently because your world model has probably matured more.

When reading, annotate connections from previous knowledge, unanswered questions and unjustified assumptions. Make flashcards of facts/quotes that you wish to analyze. Do a write-up in your own words. Revisit arguments that weren't convincing.

Collect questions and counter-arguments into a ‘questions’ basket. Answer questions from this basket in your free time.

The basket should have a hard cap, e.g. 20 questions. Picking a question at random to investigate is a nice way of staying motivated. I should not hesitate to cut off questions that I'm no longer interested in.
