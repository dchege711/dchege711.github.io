Computers Can Solve Your Problem. You May Not Like the Answer. [Boston Globe]

Dated Sep 21, 2018; last modified on Sat, 21 Mar 2020

Computers Can Solve Your Problem. You May Not Like the Answer. What happened when Boston Public Schools tried for equity with an algorithm. David Scharfenberg. The Boston Globe. https://apps.bostonglobe.com/ideas/graphics/2018/09/equity-machine/. 2018-09-21.

Teens need their sleep: sleep-deprived teens are at increased risk for poor academic performance, binge drinking, and suicide.

BPS laid out the algorithm’s four guiding principles:

  • Increase the number of high school students starting school after 8am
  • Decrease the number of elementary school students dismissed after 4pm
  • Accommodate the needs of special education students wherever possible
  • Generate transportation savings that could be reinvested in the schools

Officials picked a solution that attempted to balance several goals — student health, cost savings, and parental happiness.

But no one anticipated the opposition. Angry parents signed an online petition and filled the school committee chamber. The city summarily dropped it.

Pushing high school start times back requires pushing elementary and middle schools into earlier time slots, some elementary schools by as much as two hours.

The algorithm promised racial equity - distributing the best school start times more evenly across white, black, and brown neighborhoods. And here were the most active, politically connected families in the city trying to spike it.

Even if the algorithm promised to reduce inequities, the upheaval involved — with nearly 85% of the district getting new start times — would hit black and brown families especially hard. Parents of color are disproportionately likely to have lower-wage jobs that will make it harder for them to change schedules to meet the new demands of BPS, let alone pay more money for additional child care after school

It was people who made the final call. This was a fundamentally human conflict, and all the computing power in the world couldn’t solve it.
