[Summary] (Paul Graham) The Age of the Essay

Dated Sep 1, 2004; last modified on Wed, 22 Jan 2020

The Age of the Essay. Paul Graham. http://www.paulgraham.com/essay.html. Sept 2004.

An essay is something that you write to try and figure out something. By definition, you can't begin with a statement.

Expressing ideas helps you form them. Writing something that others might read forces you to think well.

Think of the essay as an idea graph. At each node, contrary to Geoffrey's Principle of Least Astonishment for UIs, branch out to the most interesting related idea.

Tips on generating these surprises:

  • Write about things that you've thought of a lot.
  • Observe a lot. The more anomalies you've seen, the more easily you'll notice new ones.
