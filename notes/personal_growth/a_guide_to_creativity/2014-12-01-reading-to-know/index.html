[Summary] (Heaton, Graham) How to Read and Know

Dated Dec 1, 2014; last modified on Wed, 22 Jan 2020

How to Read. Robert Heaton. https://robertheaton.com/2018/06/25/how-to-read/. https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=17424652. June 25th, 2018.
Know. Paul Graham. http://www.paulgraham.com/know.html. Dec 2014.
  • Choose your content. Some books are not worth the effort.
  • Reading and experience train your model of the world. Your mind is like a compiled program for which you've lost the source code.
  • At a different time, re-reading the book will be compiled differently because your world model has probably matured more.

Annotations to make when reading:

  • Connections from previous knowledge
  • Unanswered questions and unjustified assumptions.

Note down:

  • Facts that you wish to eternalize in a flashcard.
  • Arguments that need to be revisited during the write-up phase.
  • Memorable quotes that you want to add to you arsenal.

Collect questions and counter-arguments into a ‘questions’ basket. Answer questions from this basket in your free time.
