[Summary] (Yudkowsky) Lotteries

Dated Feb 1, 2015; last modified on Sat, 21 Mar 2020

Lotteries: A Waste of Hope. Eliezer Yudkowsky. Rationality: From AI to Zombies. Feb 2015.
New Improved Lottery. Eliezer Yudkowsky. Rationality: From AI to Zombies. Feb 2015.

The lottery is a sink of emotional energy. It encourages people to invest in their dreams, their hopes for a better future, into an infinitesimal probability.

People can't devalue the emotional force of pleasant anticipation by a factor of .0000001 without dropping the line of reasoning entirely. They usually treat the calculated utility as one argument to be balanced against their pleasant anticipations. Ideally, the calculations should override, not balance.

Suggestion: Let the lottery pay out every 5 years on average, at a random time. That way, you buy in once, and get a few years of epsilon chance of becoming rich!

  • Yes, offering people tempting dreams that will not actually happen sure is a valuable service. People are willing to pay; it must be valuable. The alternative is that consumers are making mistakes, and we all know that can't happen.
