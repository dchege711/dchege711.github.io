[Summary] (Less Wrong) I Want It My Way

Dated Jan 4, 2019; last modified on Wed, 22 Jan 2020

I Want It My Way!. Nick Hayes. Less Wrong. https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/WiDh3ca3C7GgrNXAn/i-want-it-my-way. 4th Jan, 2019.

Letting go of how you think things should be lets you see how things are. Consequently, you'll be able to get better results.

Wanting it my way: I believe that failure is bad, and that if I fail, then I personally am I failure. So I have those two things conflated. Then anything I do risks failing, especially new things, so I'd better not do the new things, then.

Remedies:

  • Keep doing small things at the boundaries, to see my own edges and expand myself.
  • Embrace the principles of MVP. Only then will I truly learn and grow.

Compassionately spend time in the not-knowing, when working in new domains. In the end I have to let go of something, in order to have something new.
