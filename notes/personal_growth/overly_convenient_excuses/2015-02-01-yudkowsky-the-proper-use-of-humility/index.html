[Summary] (Yudkowsky) The Proper Use of Humility

Dated Feb 1, 2015; last modified on Wed, 22 Jan 2020

The Proper Use of Humility. Eliezer Yudkowsky. Rationality: From AI to Zombies. Feb 2015.

To be humble is to take specific actions in anticipation of your own errors. To confess your fallibility and then do nothing about it is not humble; it is boasting of your modesty.

Good humility: Engineer humbly designs fail-safe mechanisms into machinery, even though they are damn sure the machinery won't fail.

Bad humility: But who can really know whether evolution is correct? It is just a theory. Do not refuse to integrate massive weights of evidence in favor of an uncomfortable conclusion.
