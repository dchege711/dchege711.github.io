Dated Nov 20, 2019; last modified on Wed, 22 Jan 2020

  • Historicism and Purposefulism in Therapy
Book Review: All Therapy Books. Scott Alexander. Slate Star Codex. https://slatestarcodex.com/2019/11/20/book-review-all-therapy-books/.

Scott's thesis: Each of the therapy books on their own can be convincing. But they should be taken in the context of All Therapy Books, which as a category are pretty worrying.

Why would new theories boast of superior performance? Hypotheses:

  • Only the best therapists come up and practice new therapies.
  • The placebo effect fails as it loses its luster.
  • The first studies are done quickly by true believers who have all the hype.
  • Patients are inoculated against the therapy's ideas after it spreads.

‘Historicism’ and Purposefulism in Therapy

Historicism: current symptoms are a manifestation (e.g. passive residue, maladaptive learning) of past traumas. Granted, many phobias, depressions and PTSD are historic, some therapies overextrapolate patient history.

Some therapies even go into purposefulism: symptoms serve some quasi-logical purpose relating to the life history, e.g. Bob fails at work to send a message to his father - as opposed to causes such as incompetence, poor social skills or ADHD. Remember how psychologists were confident of distant mothers causing autism while overbearing ones caused schizophrenia?
