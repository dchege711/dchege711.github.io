Why are you going to school? To get an 'education' ending in a 'degree'. Blank out the forbidden words and their synonyms, visualize the details and you'll notice that 'school' seems to consist of sitting next to bored people listening to material that you already know, that a 'degree' is a piece of paper with some writing on it, and that 'education' is forgetting the material as soon as you're tested on it.

 

The strongest definitions use a crossfire of intensional (using other words) and extensional (pointing to examples) communication to nail down a concept. Even so, you can only communicate maps to concepts, or instructions for building concepts - you don't communicate the actual categories as they exist in your mind or in the world.

 

 

 

Naïve Bayes. Perhaps, given that a thing is 'human', then the probabilities of it speaking, wearing clothes, and having the standard number of fingers, are all independent. Although this reasoning usually isn't quite true, pretending it's true can simplify the living daylights out of your calculations without you being way off.

 

Choose your definitions wisely. The mere act of creating a word can cause your mind to allocate a category, and thereby trigger unconscious inferences of similarity.

 

To categorize is to lose some information. Things given the same name end up dumped in the same mental bucket, blurring them together into the same point on the map.

 

You come up with a list of things that feel similar, and take a guess at why this is so. But when you discover what they really have in common, be prepared to revise your initial guess and even your list. It's illegal to refuse to condition prior beliefs on new evidence.

 

But an effect can become a cause, reinforcing the original cause and producing the same effect in intensified form. The language becomes ugly and inaccurate because our thoughts are foolish, but the slovenliness of our language makes it easy for us to have foolish thoughts. The point is that the process is reversible.

 

 

 

 

 

If a metaphor doesn't evoke an image, don't use it. Tired/Dying metapahors are a lazy man's game.

 

A word itself can have the destructive force of cliché; a word itself can carry the poison of a cached thought.

 

We need to let the meaning choose the word, and not the other way round. It's better to put off using words as long as possible and getting one's meaning as clear as one can using pictures and sensations.

 

The first line of defense is not to define your problematic terms, but to see whether you can think without using those terms at all.

 

Astroturfing. Companies forming good-sounding non-profits, e.g. Citizens for Fire Safety, that don't have to disclose their donors. Last Week Tonight Episode on the same.



See also Beautifully Misnamed Bills in Congress.



Look at a region. If PR companies are making bank, look out for their fingerprints.

 

 

 

 

Passive voice obscures reality. "The subjects were administered Progenitorivox". In reality, the scientist is there, and the subjects are students and the Progenitorivox was handed over with instructions.

 

Why do people misuse language?

 

  • Standard phrases already have rhythm.
  • Pretentious diction to make the banal appear profound, the biased seem scientifically impartial and sordid events masquerade as dignified.
  • Some words are not meant to point to any discoverable object, e.g. praising a country as democratic

 

Meaning does not excuse impact. Be consciously aware of the experiences that words create. "AI should be developed through democratic processes" can't be excused from signaling the audience to applaud. Furthermore, it's as vague as they come.

 

 

 

If you simplify your English, you are freed from the worst follies of orthodoxy. You can't speak of any dialects, and when you make a stupid remark, its stupidity will be obvious, even to yourself.

 

 

Political language has come to consist mainly of euphemism, question-begging and sheer cloudy vagueness. Defenseless villages are bombarded from the air, inhabitants driven from the countryside, the cattle are machine-gunned, the huts are set on fire with incendiary bullets: this is called PACIFICATION. See also TRANSFER OF POPULATION, ELIMINATION OF UNDESIRABLE ELEMENTS

 

 

 

The Parable of Hemlock. "All men are mortal, Socrates is a man, therefore Socrates is mortal. It is not merely a guess, but a logical certainty." And they waited, and they waited, and they waited… "Socrates appears not to be mortal. Then Socrates must not be a man. And that is not merely a guess, but a logical certainty. But by their definition, we cannot call Socrates a man until after we observe his to be mortal.

 

The Parable of the Dagger. The first box was inscribed: Either both inscriptions are true, or both transcriptions are false. And the second box was inscribed: This box contains the key. The jester opened the second box and found a dagger. "It's logically impossible!" "It's entirely possible. I merely wrote those inscriptions on two boxes, and then I put the dagger in the second one.

 

Disguised Queries. This object may be purple, but it's still egg-shaped, furred, flexible and opaque, like all other bleggs. Bleggs are blue and egg-shaped, rubes are red and cube-shaped. Incidentally, it may interest you that bleggs contain small nuggets of vanadium ore, and rubes contain shreds of palladium, both of which are useful industrially.

 

 

 

 

Is the number 29,384,209 prime? By virtue of how I define my decimal system and my axioms of arithmetic, I have already determined my answer to the question - but I do not know what the answer is yet, and I must do some logic to find out.

 

"If a tree falls in the forest, and no one hears it, does it make a sound?" If you ask, "Are there acoustic vibrations?" or "Are there auditory experiences", you discover that the argument is really about the definition of the word 'sound'
