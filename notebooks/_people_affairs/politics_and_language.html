(04/2002: Michael Crichton) Why Speculate?

Nobody knows what the future holds, even with immediate predictions, e.g. if minimum wage gets raised…

On the long haul, pundits do no better than chance. Previously, pundits provided context about what had already happened, a legitimate function of expertise. Nowadays, they discuss what may happen yet they have no benefit of expertise. Read futurists' magazines from a couple of years ago and realize they don't know more about the future than you or

Unwarranted credibility: simply appearing in the media provides credibility. Murray Gell-Mann Effect: Open the newspaper to an article on some subject that you know well. Often, the article is so wrong it actually reverses cause and effect. In any case, you read with exasperation/amusement. You then turn the page to international affairs and read as if the rest of the newspaper was somehow more accurate about Palestine than the baloney you just read.

Speculation is cheap, and most importantly, you cannot lose. You're wrong at least 50% of the time, but nobody remembers and therefore nobody cares. Beware of the unknowable/hard questions since speculation can't be disproven, e.g. origin of life on earth, SETI, string theory .

What's wrong with media's speculation craze? Tendency to be excessive in order to make drama. Events aren't compelling, but they need to be made into a crisis. People might think that conflicts IRL also need to be polarized. Hysteria (Y2K) and hasty litigation (breast implants).

Naïve Bayes. Perhaps, given that a thing is 'human', then the probabilities of it speaking, wearing clothes, and having the standard number of fingers, are all independent. Although this reasoning usually isn't quite true, pretending it's true can simplify the living daylights out of your calculations without you being way off. It's illegal to refuse to condition prior beliefs on new evidence.

Choose your definitions wisely. The mere act of creating a word can cause your mind to allocate a category, and thereby trigger unconscious inferences of similarity. To categorize is to lose some information. Things given the same name end up dumped in the same mental bucket, blurring them together into the same point on the map.

The strongest definitions use a crossfire of intensional (using other words) and extensional (pointing to examples) communication to nail down a concept. Even so, you can only communicate maps to concepts, or instructions for building concepts - you don't communicate the actual categories as they exist in your mind or in the world.

The Parable of Hemlock. " All men are mortal, Socrates is a man, therefore Socrates is mortal. It is not merely a guess, but a logical certainty." And they waited, and they waited, and they waited… "Socrates appears not to be mortal. Then Socrates must not be a man. And that is not merely a guess, but a logical certainty. But by their definition, we cannot call Socrates a man until after we observe his to be mortal.

The Parable of the Dagger. The first box was inscribed: Either both inscriptions are true, or both transcriptions are false. And the second box was inscribed: This box contains the key. The jester opened the second box and found a dagger. "It's logically impossible!" "It's entirely possible. (04/1946: Orwell) Politics and the English Language

But an effect can become a cause, reinforcing the original cause and producing the same effect in intensified form. The language becomes ugly and inaccurate because our thoughts are foolish, but the slovenliness of our language makes it easy for us to have foolish thoughts. The point is that the process is reversible.

Standard phrases already have rhythm. They are a convenient shortcut for lazy communicators.

Pretentious diction to make the banal appear profound, the biased seem scientifically impartial and sordid events masquerade as dignified.

Some words are not meant to point to any discoverable object, e.g. praising a country as democratic

We need to let the meaning choose the word, and not the other way round. The first line of defense is not to define your problematic terms, but to see whether you can think without using those terms at all.

If you simplify your English, you are freed from the worst follies of orthodoxy. You can't speak of any dialects, and when you make a stupid remark, its stupidity will be obvious, even to yourself.

Passive voice obscures reality. "The subjects were administered Progenitorivox". In reality, the scientist is there, and the subjects are students and the Progenitorivox was handed over with instructions.

If a metaphor doesn't evoke an image, don't use it. Tired/Dying metapahors are a lazy man's game. A word itself can have the destructive force of cliché; a word itself can carry the poison of a cached thought.

Meaning does not excuse impact. Be consciously aware of the experiences that words create. "AI should be developed through democratic processes" can't be excused from signaling the audience to applaud. Furthermore, it's as vague as they come.

Political language has come to consist mainly of euphemism, question-begging and sheer cloudy vagueness. Defenseless villages are bombarded from the air, inhabitants driven from the countryside, the cattle are machine-gunned, the huts are set on fire with incendiary bullets: this is called PACIFICATION.
