08/2017 (Epsilon Theory): The Political Meta-Game

 

(The Death of Cooperative Game-Playing in American Politics)

 

In truth, no one cares about Confederate war memorials. There's no 'Southern Identity' associated with Robert E. Lee statues. It's not like you're canceling an SEC football game.

 

But tell Southerners that "THEY are coming to take your statues away from you". Elected Republicans must come out in favor of the statues. Elected Democrats must take action to dismantle statues.

 

Trump's tweets and associated narrative construction are a dog whistle for Democrats and an obedience collar for the Republicans.

 

Politics is the mind killer

 

Arguments are soldiers. Once you know which side you're on, you must support all arguments of that side, and attack all the arguments that appear to favor the enemy side; otherwise it's like stabbing your soldiers in the back.

 

Reversed stupidity is not intelligence.

 

A hundred inventors failing to build flying machines using metal, wood and canvas doesn't imply that what you really need is a flying machine made of bones and flesh. A fool can't be wrong 99.9% of the time, unless they intelligent enough to always pick the wrong answer.

 

Correspondence Bias

 

Tendency to draw inferences on a person's dispositions from behaviors that can be entirely explained by the situations in which they occur, e.g. He's kicking the vending machine - he must be an 'angry person'. Oh, I'm kicking the fucking vending machine because the bus was late and the train was early & the stupid machine has eaten my lunch money for the 2nd time in a row.

 

Fundamental Attribution Error

 

Tendency to over-attribute others' behavior to their dispositions, while reversing this tendency for ourselves.

 

Are your enemies innately evil?

 

Everyone is the hero of their own story. It's a more inspiring battle cry to scream 'Die, vicious scum!' instead of 'Die, people who could have been just like me but grew up in a different environment.

 

How people deal with cognitive bias

 

Change one's view of the facts, e.g. deny that unfair events took place, or edit history to make it appear fair.

 

Change one's mentality - deny that the events are unfair.

 

Affect Heuristic

 

When subjective impressions of goodness/badness act as a heuristic, e.g. "a reactor design produces less waste, therefore its probability of meltdown is lower", or "Stalin also says X. You wouldn't want to agree with Stalin, would you?

 

Just World Fallacy

 

Tendency to attribute consequences to a universal force that restores moral balance.

 

Hug the Query

 

Learning about biases may make one forget to examine direct arguments. When its impossible to hug all the queries, at least be aware that you're clinging onto an approximation.

 

Argument screens off authority

 

Given only claims of a flying machine, I'd trust Lord Kelvin (he might know of counterevidence) over Wright Brothers' words.

 

If I see the Wrights' calculations, Lord Kelvin's authority becomes less relevant. If I watch the plane fly, who cares what Lord Kelvin is?
