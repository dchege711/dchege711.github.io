11/2018 (Reddit): Outliers Dominate the Internet

  • In common literature:
    • The Internet's 1% Rule: Only 1% of users actively create content; the rest only lurk
    • Pareto Principle: 20% of a group will produce 80% of the activity
    • Power Law Distribution: Level of inequality gets more drastic as you examine smaller subsets
  • The largest Reddit subs see from 1% to 3% of unique subscribers comment per month.
  • 0.2% of Wikipedia's 32m actively contribute. Furthermore, 0.003% of the 32m contribute 66% of the edits.

10/218: (Housel) No One Is Crazy

People can be misinformed, have incomplete information, be bad at math, be persuaded by rotten marketing, misjudge the consequences of their actions but their decisions make sense to them in that moment and check all the boxes they need to check.

The cornerstone of behavioral finance is that most people assume it's a field whose documented flaws apply to other people, but not themselves.

"Much of the stuff that you either have now, or have a good chance of getting, we don't. Buying a lottery ticket is the only time in our lives we can hold a tangible dream of getting the good stuff that you already have and take for granted. We are paying for a dream, and you may not understand because you are already living in a dream."

Few 1999 investors were greedy. Most didn't actually think the discounted future cash flows of Cisco Systems were worth $600b. They thought Cisco stock would go up next week.

A barbell personality with confidence on one side and paranoia on the other; willing to make bold moves but always within the context of making survival the top priority.

10/2018: (Steve Lagerfeld) In with the Out Crowd: Contrarians, Alone and Together

Whether you’re a Satanist or an alt-right activist, you sign up for a total lifestyle package that includes a limited menu of approved ideas, clothing styles, and other badges you can choose from to express your individuality. What you get in return is an intense sense of belonging and identity—we’re all pariahs here!

A contrarian is by definition someone with a singular idea who stands against the crowd. He or she takes a risk. August Landmesser risked everything. (He escaped punishment for his act of defiance but later served time in a concentration camp and was killed in action in 1944 while serving in a penal battalion.)

Today's risk is social: They risk the disapproval of the crowd - of their friends, family, colleagues, community, and society. They might simply face unspoken disapproval, or they might be shunned and ostracized or burned at the stake of Twitter. Some face criticism and censure or social or professional excommunication. They risk their status and prestige. Some risk losing their jobs.

Over the years, I’ve gone from thinking of my instinctive desire to be a minority of one as a distinguishing trait to thinking of it as something more like Asperger’s syndrome—a disability that can in rare circumstances be an advantage.

If the whole point of belonging to the crowd is to demonstrate one’s superior moral position, an insider’s dissent is an intolerable violation and contradiction:

  • Amy Wax, the UPenn law professor praising bourgeois culture and declaring that “all cultures are not equal.
  • Bob Inglis, the former Republican congressman from Georgia who committed the crime of taking climate change seriously
  • Ellen Pao, who upended the heroically contrarian self-image of Silicon Valley by suing her locally fabled employer, Kleiner Perkins Caufield & Byers, for the all-too-mainstream practice of sex discrimination
  • Bennet Omalu, the Pittsburgh forensic pathologist who first linked NFL players’ head injuries to degenerative brain disease
  • Kate Roiphe, the feminist who dared to question the #MeToo movement.

07/2018: (Morgan Housel) Real World vs. Book Knowledge

Book Knowledge: People tend to avoid loss more enthusiastically than equivalent gains. Real World Knowledge: Try to determine how much he's overpaying for loss aversion, e.g. medical bills, kid in college, etc.

A student knows the rules. A master knows the exceptions. Learn the rules and then spend as much time observing how people use and abuse those rules. Read outside your field to see how people respond to incentives, think about risk, and deal with surprises.

10/2017: (Ben Hunt) Sheep Logic

The Logic of the Flock

  • Enormous capacity for other-regarding behavior. This doesn't imply other-obliged. A wolf can die for its pack. A flock member won't.
  • Zero altruism and overwhelming foolishness
  • Determination to pursue behaviors that satisfy #1 and #2 to their extremes.

The 2 most powerful other-regarding emotions that we have are schadenfreude (feeling happy when others are sad) and jealousy.

Jukebox Effect: Showing you what others think encourages flock behavior.
