11/2018 (DHH) You Probably Won't Make it to the Top

Fallacy: If you really set your mind to it, you can do it. Get to the very top. Beat out all the others. That’s how I did it. I just wanted it more, worked hard, and eventually all my dreams came true. I won’t say it’s easy, but it’s up to you!

Odds are overwhelmingly that you will not make it to the top. There just isn’t enough room up there, on the top. Why give simultaneous false hope and a false idol to so many when you know that it just isn’t going to happen for the vast majority?

The top is full of people who hate what they had to do or who they had to become to get there. Even for the people who get there with a clean conscience often end up disappointed by how shallow the satisfaction really is.

Stop playing games where you can’t set the rules. Start winning the ones where you can. But you do have control over whether you’re doing a good job, as measured by your personal sense of satisfaction in the work.

10/2018 (Housel): Humble Exits

Jack Welch declined $110m for a 10th season of Seinfeld because he was done with the show. He wanted to end the show on the same kind of peak.

The show was based on recreating real events from his and Larry David's normal life. They were running out of material because it had been so long since they experienced a normal life.

Quitting or adjusting paths on top is hard because no one knows where the top is. If your success pushes you away from doing things that had been fundamental to that success, you're probably at the top!

Learn when to admit failure.

It's important to have a watershed moment, the moment of humbling realization. To acknowledge a fundamental problem, not to divide it into palatable bite-size mistakes.

Not all mistakes are disguised successes. Don't paint all failures as learning experiences.

LTCM were exploiting market inefficiencies. Other people learned of the tricks. LTCM didn't lose hope. They borrowed more and more leverage to exploit tinier and tinier margins.

Cowards live to fight another day.

It takes less courage to visualize an uncomfortable state of affairs as a thought experiment than to consider how likely it is to be true.

Yes, people do get over things. Newly minted quadriplegics are not as sad 6 months later as they expected to be.

Be a true coward. Plan out and visualize every step of your retreat before you first come to battle.

Consider a Crisis of Faith When:

  • A belief has long remained in your mind.
  • It is surrounded by a cloud of known arguments and refutations.
  • You have sunk costs in it (time, public declarations)
  • The belief has emotional consequences.
  • The belief has gotten mixed up with your personality.
