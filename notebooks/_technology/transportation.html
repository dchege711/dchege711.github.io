(02/2019: SoECS) Smart Cities

Traditional city data: transit use, energy and utilities, public utilities e.g. Wi-Fi. Case Study: NYC Open Data

Smart cities centralize data collection and optimize municipal services, e.g. redirect traffic, change parking costs, transit routes and schedules, EV charging stations, peak pricing for utilities, electronic cards for facilities, garbage collection routes (See Braess's Paradox )

Case Study: Toronto Quayside by Sidewalk Labs, a smart city from the ground up.

  • Proposals: reduce emissions, engineered wood, mixed-use streets, underground tunnels for waste management and freight delivery, centralized ID system
  • Principles: only collect necessary data, discard after use, consent needed for identifying data, make anonymized data open to the public
    • Anonymized data isn't foolproof, especially when you can combine related datasets where one has personal info
  • Is choosing not to live in a smart city the only genuine opt-out option?

2015/07: Human Transit: The Transit Ridership Recipe

The Ridership vs Coverage Problem

Low-ridership and high-coverage systems make little business sense, but may be necessary because some feel entitled to service (e.g. paying taxes) or badly needs the service (e.g. We'll be trapped otherwise)

Designing a Ridership Maximizing Transit System

The elapsed time between consecutive buses matter. High frequency reduces wait time, makes connections easy, and is a backstop for problems of reliability.

As transit ridership goes up, responsiveness to each individual's desires and needs goes down. The big-city subway doesn't take care of your personal needs, but that's inseparable from why it's massively liberating to so many people.

Suppose we fix frequency and speed of travel constant. How far do we have to go to be available to a given number of people? We'll consider:

  • The number of people, jobs and activities near each stop (denser areas win)
  • Is it possible to walk between the stop and activities around it (grid-like areas win)
  • How direct does the trip feel? (like how you feel bored stopping at Lakeside instead of directly at the Wa)
