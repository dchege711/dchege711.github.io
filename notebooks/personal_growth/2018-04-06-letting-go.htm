Cowards live to fight another day.

 

It takes less courage to visualize an uncomfortable state of affairs as a thought experiment than to consider how likely it is to be true.

 

Yes, people do get over things. Newly minted quadriplegics are not as sad 6 months later as they expected to be.

 

Be a true coward. Plan out and visualize every step of your retreat before you first come to battle.

 

Learn when to admit failure.

 

It's important to have a watershed moment, the moment of humbling realization. To acknowledge a fundamental problem, not to divide it into palatable bite-size mistakes.

 

Not all mistakes are disguised successes. Don't paint all failures as learning experiences.

 

LTCM were exploiting market inefficiencies. Other people learned of the tricks. LTCM didn't lose hope. They borrowed more and more leverage to exploit tinier and tinier margins.

 

 

Consider a Crisis of Faith When:

 

  • A belief has long remained in your mind.
  • It is surrounded by a cloud of known arguments and refutations.
  • You have sunk costs in it (time, public declarations)
  • The belief has emotional consequences.
  • The belief has gotten mixed up with your personality.
